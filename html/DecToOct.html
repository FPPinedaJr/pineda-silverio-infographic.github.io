How to Convert from Decimal to Octal

Step 1.

Pick any decimal number to start with. For example, 67010.

Step 2.

Divide this amount by 8. For now disregard the remainder and divide the decimal number by 8 so that it can be converted to octal.

  • In this case, 670 / 8 equals 83.

Step 3.

Find the remainder. The minimal number remaining after we've "counted by 8" as many times as we can is the remainder is the octal number's final digit, located in the ones location.

  • In this case, 670 / 8 equals 83, leaving a remainder of 6.
  • So far, the octal number is 6.

Step 4.

Divide the result of your division by 8. Divide your result by 8 once more. Make a note of the result, then find the remainder.

  • In our illustration: 83 was the solution to our last division.
  • 83 / 8 equals 10 remainder 3
  • So far, our octal number is 36.

Step 5.

Once more divide by 8. Divide the solution to your previous division once more. Find the remaining amount by dividing it by 8 once more.

  • For instance: The solution to our most recent division was 10.
  • 10 / 8 = 1 remainder 2
  • So far, our octal number is 236.

Step 6.

Step 6. Continue until you discover the last digit. Your final division calculation will yield a result of 0. The decimal number has now undergone a complete conversion.

  • In our example: The answer to our last division was 1.

  • Our final answer is the octal number 1236. We can write this as 12368 to show that it is an octal number.

Step 7. Recognize how this technique works. Here is an explanation if you're having problems comprehending this technique.

  • Start with any decipal.
  • Divide the decimal with 8 to convert it to octal.
  • Take note of the remainder, that is a digit for the octal.
  • This continues until you discover the entire octal number.

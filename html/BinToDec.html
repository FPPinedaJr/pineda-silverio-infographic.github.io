How to Convert from Binary to Decimal

Step 1.

Write down the binary number, then list the powers of 2 in order from right to left. To convert 110110112 from binary to decimal. Write it down first. Next, list the powers of two going from right to left. Start at 20 and give it a "1" evaluation. Exponents are increased by one for each power. Stop when the list's elements are the same number of digits as the binary number. Eight digits make up the sample number, 11011011, thus the list would have the following eight items: 128, 64, 32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 1.

Step 2.

On the top are the powers of two for each of the binary number's digits.To make each binary digit correspond to its power of two, just put 11011011 below the digits 128. 64. 32. 16. 8. 4. 2. and 1. The binary number and the stated powers of two should match up with the binary number. If you choose, you may also write the binary digits above the powers of two, what matters most is that they line up.

Step 3.

Times each power of two to its corresponding binary digit. Go from one binary digit to the next. The power of two for the digit, if it is a 1, the value should be written below the line, underneath the digit. Write a 0 below the line, under the digit, if it is a 0.

  • 1 times 1 equals 1.
  • 2 times 1 equals 2.
  • 4 times 0 equals 0.
  • 8 times 1 equals 8.
  • 16 times 1 equals 16.
  • 32 times 0 equals 0.
  • 64 times 1 equals 64.
  • 128 times 1 equals 128.

Step 4.

Sum up the computed values. Add the numbers that are listed below the line. Add 128 + 64 + 0 + 16 + 8 + 0 + 2 + 1 = 219. This is the binary number 11011011's decimal conversion.

Step 5.

Write the answer along with its correct base subscript. Now, all you have to do to demonstrate that you are using a decimal answer that must be operating in powers of 10, so write 219 with base 10.

How to Convert Octal to Binary

Step 1.

Write the given octal. Write the given octal and seperate each digit.

Step 2.

Write the binary bits for each digit. In the case above, 2 sets of 3 binary bits are needed to convert the octal. Thus two sets of 22, 21, 20 or just simply 4,2,1 are needed. In other cases:

  • 1238, three sets of 4,2,1 are needed.
  • 777778, five sets of 4,2,1 are needed.

Step 3.

Compute for the needed binary bits. Using the previous example, 258:

  • For the digit 2: a bit of 2 is needed.
  • For the digit 5: a bit of 4 and also a bit of 1 are needed to make up the digit 5.

Step 4.

Format the binary. Simply remove the space between the comuted binary digits and remove any unnecessary "0" from the left if there is any. In the example just join the 010 101 and remove the 0 from the left making 10101.

Step 5.

Write the answer along with its correct base subscript. Now, all you have to do to show that you are using a binary answer that must be operating in powers of 2, so write 10101 with base 2.

How to Convert from Decimal to Hexadecimal

Step 1.

Write down the decimal number. Write down any decimal number. For example, 91010.

Step 2.

Divide the number by 16. Divide the decimal number by 16 so that it can be converted to hexadecimal.

  • In the example, it will be 910 /16 equals 56 remainder 14.

Step 3.

Continue until you meet zero. Continue repeating the division by 16 until your final division yields a result of 0.

  • 56 / 16 = 3 r.8
  • 3 / 16 = 0 r.3

Step 4.

Construct the hexadecimal number. Make the hexadecimal number by joining the computed remainders from bottom to top.

Note: First, convert the 10 and above decimal number to hexadecimal as shown in the table below:

Decimal 10 11 12 13 14 15
Hexadecimal A B C D E F

For the example, 14 needs to be converted to E first before joining the hexadecimal values making 38E.

Step 5.

Write the answer along with its correct base subscript. Now, all you have to do to show that you are using a hexadecimal answer that must be operating in powers of 16, so write 38E with base 16.

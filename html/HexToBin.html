How to Convert Hexadecimal to Binary

Step 1.

Write the given hexadecimal. Write the given hexadecimal and seperate each digit.

Step 2.

Write the binary bits for each digit. In the case above, 3 sets of 4 binary bits are needed to convert the hexadecimal. Thus three sets of 23, 22, 21, 20 or just simply 8,4,2,1 are needed.

Step 3.

Compute for the needed binary bits. Using the previous example, 1AC16:

  • For the digit 1: a bit of 1 is needed.
  • For the digit A or 10: a bit of 8 and also a bit of 2 are needed to make up the digit 10.
  • For the digit C or 12: a bit of 8 and also a bit of 4 are needed to make up the digit 12.

Note: First, convert the given hexadecimal letters based on the table below:

Decimal 10 11 12 13 14 15
Hexadecimal A B C D E F

Step 4.

Format the binary. Simply remove the space between the comuted binary digits and remove any unnecessary "0" from the left if there is any. In the example just join the 0001 1010 1100 and remove the unnecessary "0" from the left making 110101100.

Step 5.

Write the answer along with its correct base subscript. Now, all you have to do to show that you are using a binary answer that must be operating in powers of 2, so write 110101100 with base 2.

How to Convert Binary to Octal Number

Step 1.

Recognize a binary number series. Simple strings of 1s and 0s make up binary integers, such as 101001, 001, or even just 1. This kind of string is typically binary if you see it. To avoid misunderstanding with the number "one thousand and one", some publications and professors additionally mark binary numerals with a subscript "2" such as 10012.

  • This subscript denotes the "base" of the number. Binary is a base-two system, octal is base-eight.

Step 2.

Starting from the far right, arrange all of the 1s and 0s in the binary number into sets of three. There are only eight octal numbers and two different binary numerals. Each octal number will require three binary numbers to represent it. To create your groups, start on the right. For instance, the binary value of 10011011 would be 10 011 011.

Step 3.

If you don't have enough digits to form a set of three, move zeros to the left of the last digit. Despite not being a multiple of three, the binary number 10011011 includes eight digits, which can be converted to octal. Simply increase your front group's zeros until it has three spots. For instance.

  • Original Binary: 10011011
  • Grouping: 10 011 011
  • Adding Zeros for Groups of Three: 010 011 011

Step 4.

Step 4. Under each group of three digits, write a 4, a 2, and a 1 as placeholders. A place in the octal number system is represented by each of a set's three binary digits. A 4, a 2, and a 1 are indicated by the first number. Write these numbers beneath your sets of three binary numbers to keep things organized. For instance:

  • 010 011 011
    421 421 421

  • 001
    421

  • 110 010 001
    421 421 421

Step 5.

Write that number (4 or 2 or 1) to begin your octal numbers if there is a one above any of your placeholders. Your octal number has a 4 if there is a one above the "4". The octal number does not include a one if there is a 0 above the location where it should be, thus leave it as zero.

  • Problem:
    • Convert 0100110112 to octal.
  • Separate into threes:
    • 010 011 011
  • Add placeholders:
    • 010 011 011
      421 421 421
  • Mark each places:
    • 010 011 011
      421 421 421
      020 021 021

Step 6.

Each set of three new numbers should be added up. You can easily add up each group of three independently once you know how many spaces there are in the octal number. As a result, for 010, which becomes 0, 2, and 0, you get 2. Continuing the prior illustration.

  • Problem:
    • Convert 1010100112 to octal.
  • Separate, add placeholders, and mark each place:
    • 010 011 011
      421 421 421
      020 021 021
  • Add up each set of three:
    • (0+2+0)(0+2+1)(0+2+1)=2,3,3

Step 7.

Step 7. Place your newly converted answers together to form your final octal number. Splitting up the binary number was just to make solving easier -- the original number was one lone string. So, now that you've converted, put everything back together to get your final answer. That's all it takes.

  • Problem:
    • Convert 010 011 0112 to octal.
  • Separate, add placeholders, mark places, and add totals:
    • 010 011 011 2 — 3 — 3
  • Put converted numbers back together:
    • 233

Step 8.

To complete the conversion, add a subscript 8. Technically, without correct notation, it is impossible to determine whether the number 523 is an octal number or a regular base-ten number. Put a subscript 8 on your response, referring to octal as a base-8 system, to let your teacher know you've been working hard.

  • Problem:
    • Convert 100110112 to octal
  • Conversion:
    • 233
  • Final Answer:
    • 2338
